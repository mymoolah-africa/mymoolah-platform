1. Legal Characterisation

The correct legal characterisation of MyMoolah's Flash eeziCash cash-out functionality, distinguishing it from VAS product distribution.

✗ Incorrect Characterisation
MyMoolah purchases and resells Flash eeziCash voucher PINs as a Value-Added Service (VAS) product. The service is functionally identical to airtime and data distribution.
✓ Correct Characterisation
MyMoolah facilitates a wallet cash withdrawal within its TPPP/sponsor-bank framework. A one-time eeziCash PIN is generated solely as the operational credential for collecting existing wallet value as cash.
Legal Basis
The eeziCash PIN is the mechanism for executing a wallet cash withdrawal — not a standalone product. The customer's value already exists in the wallet before the PIN is generated. MyMoolah does not purchase, warehouse, or hold standalone eeziCash voucher inventory for resale to the public.

Key Assumptions

  1. The customer holds value in an existing MyMoolah wallet before the PIN is generated.
  2. The wallet forms part of MyMoolah's sponsor-bank-backed payment structure (Standard Bank).
  3. MyMoolah does not purchase, warehouse, or hold standalone eeziCash voucher inventory for resale.
  4. The eeziCash PIN is generated only after the customer gives a withdrawal instruction against existing wallet value.
  5. The PIN is single-use, time-limited, auditable, and subject to reversal if not redeemed.
  6. MyMoolah does not assume an independent repayment obligation to the public outside the wallet structure.

3. Flash eeziCash Wallet Cash-Withdrawal Flow

End-to-end flow for a MyMoolah customer withdrawing cash from their wallet through the Flash trader network.

Wallet Cash Withdrawal via Flash eeziCash PIN
1
Customer
Customer holds value in MyMoolah wallet
Funds already exist in the wallet under the Standard Bank-sponsored TPPP structure (e.g. earned wage access disbursement, EasyPay deposit, P2P transfer).
↓

Customer
Customer initiates cash withdrawal
Selects “Withdraw Cash” in the MyMoolah app, chooses an amount (e.g. R200). The disclosed transaction fee is shown before confirmation.
↓
3
MyMoolah
Wallet debited — double-entry ledger journal recorded
MyMoolah debits the customer’s wallet for the withdrawal amount (R200) plus the applicable transaction fee. A double-entry ledger journal is recorded within the TPPP/sponsor-bank framework.
↓
4
MyMoolah → Flash API
Withdrawal instruction sent to Flash Partner API
MyMoolah calls the Flash Partner API v4 to initiate the generation of a one-time eeziCash redemption PIN against the customer’s wallet withdrawal. MyMoolah does not purchase voucher inventory.
↓
5
Flash
Flash generates one-time redemption PIN
Flash generates a 15-digit one-time PIN (the operational credential for cash collection) and returns it to MyMoolah. The PIN is single-use, time-limited, and auditable.
↓
6
MyMoolah → Customer
PIN delivered to customer
MyMoolah delivers the redemption PIN to the customer via the app. The customer is shown the PIN, expiry time, withdrawal amount, and instructions.
↓
7
Customer → Flash Trader
Customer collects cash at Flash trader
Customer presents the PIN to any participating Flash trader (~240,000 locations). The trader verifies the PIN with Flash and dispenses cash.
↓
8
Flash → MyMoolah
Settlement and reconciliation
Flash settles against MyMoolah’s pre-funded float. Daily reconciliation confirms redeemed and unredeemed PINs. Comparable to cash-funding arrangements between banks and ATM networks.
Reversal Process
If the eeziCash PIN expires unredeemed, the withdrawal amount is automatically reversed to the customer’s wallet. MyMoolah retains responsibility for the withdrawn funds throughout the end-to-end process — from wallet debit through to cash collection or reversal.

Critical Distinction
The wallet debit occurs before the PIN is generated. The customer’s value already exists and is debited first. The PIN is then generated as the credential for cash collection. This sequence confirms that the service is a withdrawal mechanism, not a product purchase.

VAS vs Cash-Out Distinction
Bill payments and product purchases are genuine VAS product acquisitions — the customer is buying a new product. Cash-out is fundamentally different: the customer is retrieving their own existing funds. These two categories require different legal characterisation and should not be conflated.

7. Regulatory Framework References

Applicable South African regulatory instruments and published positions supporting the characterisation of this service as a wallet cash-withdrawal mechanism within the TPPP/sponsor-bank framework.

PASA TPPP Framework
PASA’s published framework contemplates non-bank third-party payment providers operating through a sponsoring bank where they accept or disburse payments for clients. MyMoolah is registered with PASA as a TPPP, with Standard Bank as its sponsor bank.
NPS Act, Section 7
Section 7 of the National Payment System Act 78 of 1998 regulates the acceptance of money or payment instructions for payment to third persons to whom that payment is due. Section 7(c) permits non-banks to handle such payments in accordance with directives issued by the Reserve Bank — specifically, Directive 1 of 2007.
SARB Position Paper NPS 01/2009 — Electronic Money
The South African Reserve Bank’s published position states that only registered South African banks may issue electronic money. However, non-banks may offer payment-related services in conjunction with a bank, and may operate as third-party payment service providers within Directives 1 and 2 of 2007.
Comparable Market Practice
Standard Bank, TymeBank, and FNB each offer cardless cash-out through the Flash retail network using one-time eeziCash PINs. In each case, the service is characterised and marketed as a bank account or wallet withdrawal — not as the purchase of a standalone voucher product. Flash publicly describes these services as providing “quick and easy access to cash withdrawals” and as “bringing the power of ATMs closer to customers.”
