Freedom Day observation (important)
================================================================================

Our Pain.001:       ReqdExctnDt = 2026-04-27 (Monday, what we requested)
SBSA INTAUD echoes: ReqdExctnDt = 2026-04-28 (Tuesday, what SBSA booked)

Why:  Monday 2026-04-27 is Freedom Day, a South African public holiday.
      BOL does not settle on public holidays. SBSA's processor correctly
      rolled forward to the next business day, which is Tuesday
      2026-04-28. This is graceful, correct behaviour on SBSA's side.

Implication for this test:
      FINAUD will fire after BOL's overnight cycle on Tuesday. So
      expect FINAUD in SBSA /Inbox around Wed 2026-04-29 early morning
      (~05:56 SAST based on Penny #1's pattern), and R1.00 debit/credit
      visible on both statements Wed 2026-04-29.

Implication for future work:
      Our pain001BulkBuilder does NOT currently know about SA public
      holidays. For this penny test, operator intervention via
      --exec-date would have caught it (a 2026-04-28 choice would have
      avoided the roll). For production flows with high transaction
      volumes we should make the app-level generator holiday-aware
      rather than relying on SBSA's roll-forward, for internal-ledger
      and audit-trail consistency. Tech-debt decision left to operator
      (not actioned in this session).

================================================================================
Operational lessons
================================================================================

1. Baseline-diff polling is the correct approach when pre-existing
   response files may still be in SBSA /Inbox. The original poller
   exited immediately on seeing Penny #1's FINAUD (`FINAUD=1`) and
   required a 1-line strategy change to work correctly for Penny #2.
2. Extended poller windows (≥ 2 hours) are cheap insurance for
   first-time tests. The 30-min window we used on Penny #1 would
   have missed the response if the SBSA rule had taken slightly
   longer to deploy.
3. Freedom Day roll-forward is handled gracefully by SBSA and does
   not require a resend. Good to know for future holiday dates.
