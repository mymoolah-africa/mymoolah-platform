## 1. Purpose

This Code of Ethics & Professional Conduct ("the Code") articulates the values, principles, and standards of behaviour that MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd ("the Company") expects of every person who acts on its behalf. It establishes the ethical foundation upon which the Company conducts its business as a provider of banking-grade financial services in South Africa, including digital wallet, value-added services, PayShap, USDC cross-border payments, NFC, KYC, and reconciliation operations.

The Code is not a substitute for sound judgement. It is a guide to help all persons within its scope navigate ethical decisions and conduct themselves in a manner that upholds the trust placed in the Company by its clients, regulators, partners, and the communities it serves.

## 2. Scope

This Code applies to:

- All directors, officers, and employees of MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd.
- All contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and interns.
- All persons acting on behalf of the Company in any capacity.

Where local laws or regulations impose higher standards than those set out in this Code, the higher standard shall prevail.

## 3. Core Values

The Company's operations and culture are grounded in the following core values:

| Value | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Integrity** | We are honest, transparent, and trustworthy in all our dealings. We do what is right, even when it is difficult or when no one is watching. |
| **Transparency** | We communicate openly with our clients, regulators, partners, and each other. We do not conceal material information or engage in deceptive practices. |
| **Accountability** | We take responsibility for our actions and decisions. We honour our commitments and acknowledge our mistakes. |
| **Innovation** | We embrace responsible innovation in financial technology to improve access, efficiency, and the client experience, while managing the risks that innovation introduces. |
| **Inclusion** | We are committed to building a workplace and a platform that is accessible, equitable, and respectful of the diversity of the communities we serve. |

## 4. Professional Behaviour Standards

### 4.1 General Conduct

All persons within scope shall:

1. Act with honesty, integrity, and professionalism at all times.
2. Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and the Company's policies and procedures.
3. Treat colleagues, clients, partners, and members of the public with dignity, courtesy, and respect.
4. Exercise reasonable care and diligence in the performance of their duties.
5. Avoid any conduct that could bring the Company into disrepute or undermine public confidence in the financial services sector.
6. Cooperate fully with internal and external audits, investigations, and regulatory inquiries.

### 4.2 Confidentiality

All persons within scope must safeguard the confidentiality of information obtained in the course of their duties. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to:

- Client data, transaction records, and account information.
- Proprietary business strategies, financial projections, and product plans.
- System architecture, security configurations, and technical infrastructure.
- Employee personal information.
- Regulatory correspondence and audit findings.

Confidential information shall not be disclosed to any unauthorised person, used for personal advantage, or retained after the termination of employment or engagement, except as required by law or with proper authorisation.

### 4.3 Use of Company Assets and Systems

Company assets — including technology systems, intellectual property, funds, equipment, and data — shall be used solely for legitimate business purposes. Personal use of Company assets is permitted only to the extent that it is reasonable, incidental, and does not interfere with work responsibilities, compromise security, or violate any law or policy.

The Company's information systems, including email, messaging platforms, and network infrastructure, are Company property. The Company reserves the right to monitor the use of its systems in accordance with applicable law, including the Regulation of Interception of Communications and Provision of Communication-Related Information Act 70 of 2002 (RICA).

### 4.4 Social Media and Public Communications

Persons within scope shall exercise caution and good judgement when using social media, whether in a personal or professional capacity. The following principles apply:

1. Do not disclose confidential or proprietary Company information on any social media platform.
2. Do not make statements that could reasonably be attributed to the Company unless specifically authorised to do so.
3. Do not engage in conduct on social media that is harassing, discriminatory, defamatory, or otherwise inconsistent with this Code.
4. Clearly distinguish personal views from the Company's position where there is any risk of confusion.

### 4.5 Client Interactions

All interactions with clients shall be conducted with fairness, honesty, and a commitment to treating clients fairly in accordance with regulatory expectations. Persons within scope shall:

1. Provide clients with accurate, clear, and complete information about the Company's products and services.
2. Not make misleading representations or omit material information.
3. Handle client complaints promptly, fairly, and in accordance with the Company's complaints procedures.
4. Protect client funds and data with the highest standards of care.
5. Not exploit a position of trust or a client's vulnerability for personal or commercial advantage.

## 5. Fair Dealing and Competition

### 5.1 Fair Dealing

All business transactions shall be conducted on the basis of fair dealing. Persons within scope shall not take unfair advantage of any person through manipulation, concealment, misrepresentation, or any other unfair practice.

### 5.2 Competition Law Compliance

The Company is committed to competing vigorously and fairly in the marketplace in compliance with the Competition Act 89 of 1998. The following conduct is prohibited:

1. Price-fixing, market allocation, or bid-rigging agreements with competitors.
2. Abuse of a dominant position.
3. Any exchange of competitively sensitive information with competitors, including pricing, market strategies, or client information.
4. Any other conduct that restricts or distorts competition.

Any contact with competitors that involves discussion of competitively sensitive matters must be reported to the Chief Compliance Officer immediately.

## 6. Intellectual Property

All intellectual property created by employees in the course of their employment belongs to the Company. Persons within scope shall:

1. Respect the intellectual property rights of the Company and of third parties.
2. Not use, reproduce, or distribute copyrighted material, software, or other intellectual property without proper authorisation or licence.
3. Protect the Company's proprietary technology, source code, algorithms, and trade secrets.
4. Comply with all software licence agreements and open-source licence obligations.

## 7. Conflicts of Interest

All persons within scope must avoid situations where their personal interests conflict, or may reasonably appear to conflict, with the interests of the Company. The detailed framework for the identification, disclosure, and management of conflicts of interest is set out in the Company's Conflict of Interest Policy (Policy 12). This Code requires, at a minimum, that any actual, potential, or perceived conflict of interest be disclosed promptly to the Chief Compliance Officer.

## 8. Anti-Discrimination and Harassment

### 8.1 Commitment

MyMoolah is committed to maintaining a workplace that is free from unlawful discrimination, harassment, bullying, and victimisation. Every person within scope has the right to be treated with dignity and respect.

### 8.2 Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is prohibited:

1. Discrimination on the basis of race, gender, sex, pregnancy, marital status, family responsibility, ethnic or social origin, colour, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion, HIV status, conscience, belief, political opinion, culture, language, birth, or any other ground listed in Section 9 of the Constitution or the Employment Equity Act 55 of 1998.
2. Sexual harassment, including unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favours, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.
3. Bullying, intimidation, or victimisation in any form.
4. Hate speech or any conduct that promotes hostility or violence on a prohibited ground.

### 8.3 Reporting

Any person who experiences or witnesses discrimination or harassment must report it to their line manager, the Human Resources Department, or the Chief Compliance Officer. Reports may also be made through the Company's whistleblowing channels. All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially.

## 9. Substance Abuse

Persons within scope shall not report for duty or perform work while under the influence of alcohol, illegal drugs, or any substance that impairs their ability to perform their duties safely and effectively. The use, possession, distribution, or sale of illegal substances on Company premises or during working hours is prohibited and constitutes grounds for summary dismissal.

## 10. Personal Trading Restrictions

Where an employee, director, or officer has access to material non-public information about the Company, its clients, or its partners, they are prohibited from trading in any securities or financial instruments on the basis of such information. This prohibition extends to tipping — the communication of material non-public information to any person who may trade on it.

Employees with access to transaction data, client positions, or settlement information must comply with any additional trading restrictions imposed by the Company.

## 11. Consequences of Violation

Any violation of this Code may result in disciplinary action, up to and including:

- Formal written warning.
- Suspension pending investigation.
- Summary dismissal for gross misconduct.
- Recovery of any loss suffered by the Company.
- Referral to law enforcement or regulatory authorities where applicable.
- Termination of contractual relationship (for contractors and third parties).

The severity of the action shall be proportionate to the nature and seriousness of the violation, the person's role, and any prior instances of misconduct.

## 12. Acknowledgement Requirement

All employees are required to acknowledge in writing that they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this Code. Acknowledgement shall be obtained at the time of hire and annually thereafter. A record of all acknowledgements shall be maintained by the Human Resources Department.

## 13. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Board of Directors** | Set the ethical tone. Approve the Code. Ensure accountability for its implementation. Lead by example. |
| **Chief Executive Officer** | Embody the Company's values. Ensure that ethical standards are integrated into business decisions and culture. |
| **Chief Compliance Officer** | Administer the Code. Provide guidance on ethical matters. Receive and manage reports of violations. Report to the Board. |
| **Human Resources** | Integrate the Code into recruitment, onboarding, training, and performance management. Maintain acknowledgement records. |
| **Line Managers** | Model ethical behaviour. Ensure their teams understand and comply with the Code. Address concerns promptly. |
| **All Employees** | Comply with the Code. Seek guidance when uncertain. Report violations. Uphold the Company's values. |

## 14. Monitoring and Review

This Code shall be reviewed annually, or sooner if required by changes in legislation, regulation, or business operations. The Chief Compliance Officer shall report to the Board at least annually on compliance with the Code, including any significant violations, trends, and recommendations for improvement.
