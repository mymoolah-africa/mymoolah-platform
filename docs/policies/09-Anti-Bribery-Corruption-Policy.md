## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd's zero-tolerance position on bribery and corruption in all forms. It provides a framework for the prevention, detection, and response to bribery and corrupt conduct across every aspect of the Company's operations, including its digital wallet, value-added services, PayShap, USDC cross-border, NFC, KYC, and reconciliation activities.

MyMoolah operates within a highly regulated financial services environment. The integrity of the Company's relationships with regulators, banking partners, Mojaloop network participants, suppliers, and clients is foundational to its licence to operate. Any act of bribery or corruption threatens that integrity and exposes the Company to criminal prosecution, regulatory sanction, reputational harm, and loss of strategic partnerships.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All directors, officers, and employees of MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd, regardless of seniority or function.
- All contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and interns engaged by the Company.
- All third-party agents, intermediaries, introducers, and business partners acting on behalf of or in association with MyMoolah.
- All operations, whether conducted within the Republic of South Africa or internationally (including cross-border USDC and remittance activities).

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Bribery** | The offering, promising, giving, accepting, or soliciting of an undue advantage of any value (financial or non-financial) to influence the action of an official or other person in a position of trust, in breach of their duties. |
| **Corruption** | The abuse of entrusted power for private gain, including but not limited to bribery, extortion, fraud, embezzlement, nepotism, and collusion. |
| **Facilitation Payment** | A payment made to a public official to expedite or secure the performance of a routine, non-discretionary governmental action. Such payments are illegal under South African law. |
| **Kickback** | A return of a portion of a sum received, typically as a result of coercion or a secret agreement, in exchange for favourable treatment in the awarding of contracts or business. |
| **Gift** | Any item of value provided or received without adequate consideration, including but not limited to cash, goods, travel, accommodation, entertainment, or favours. |
| **Hospitality** | The provision of food, drink, entertainment, or accommodation in a business context. |
| **Public Official** | Any person holding a legislative, administrative, or judicial office, whether appointed or elected, at any level of government, and any person exercising a public function, including for a public agency or enterprise. |
| **Third Party** | Any individual or organisation acting on behalf of or in association with MyMoolah, including agents, intermediaries, introducers, consultants, suppliers, and joint venture partners. |

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Zero-Tolerance Statement

MyMoolah adopts an absolute zero-tolerance approach to bribery and corruption. No employee, director, officer, contractor, or third party acting for or on behalf of the Company shall engage in any form of bribery or corrupt conduct, whether directly or through an intermediary, regardless of local customs, competitive pressures, or perceived business necessity.

### 4.2 Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is strictly prohibited:

1. Offering, promising, giving, or authorising the giving of any bribe, gratification, or undue advantage to any person, whether a public official or private party.
2. Requesting, agreeing to receive, or accepting any bribe, gratification, or undue advantage from any person.
3. Making or accepting facilitation payments of any kind. There is no exception to this prohibition.
4. Engaging in or facilitating kickback arrangements with suppliers, partners, or any other party.
5. Using intermediaries, agents, consultants, or any third party to circumvent the prohibitions set out in this policy.
6. Offering or accepting any gift, hospitality, or benefit that could reasonably be perceived as intended to improperly influence a business decision or official action.
7. Falsifying, misrepresenting, or concealing any record or account to disguise bribery or corrupt conduct.

### 4.3 Gifts and Hospitality

#### 4.3.1 Thresholds

| Category | Threshold (per instance) | Threshold (per recipient per annum) |
|----------|--------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Gifts given | R500 | R1,500 |
| Gifts received | R500 | R1,500 |
| Hospitality given | R1,000 | R3,000 |
| Hospitality received | R1,000 | R3,000 |

#### 4.3.2 Mandatory Requirements

- All gifts and hospitality, whether given or received, must be recorded in the Gifts & Hospitality Register maintained by the Compliance Department.
- Gifts or hospitality exceeding the per-instance threshold require prior written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer.
- Gifts or hospitality involving public officials require prior written approval from the Chief Compliance Officer regardless of value.
- Cash gifts or cash equivalents (vouchers, gift cards with no branding, cryptocurrency) are prohibited in all circumstances.
- Gifts and hospitality must be reasonable, proportionate, and made in good faith without any intention to improperly influence.

### 4.4 Political Contributions

MyMoolah does not make political contributions of any kind, whether monetary or in-kind, directly or indirectly. No employee, director, or officer is authorised to make any contribution to a political party, political candidate, or political organisation on behalf of the Company.

### 4.5 Charitable Donations and Sponsorships

All charitable donations and sponsorship arrangements must receive prior written approval from the Chief Executive Officer and the Chief Compliance Officer. Due diligence must be conducted on the recipient organisation to ensure it is a legitimate entity and that the donation or sponsorship is not used as a vehicle for corrupt conduct. No donation or sponsorship shall be made at the request of, or for the benefit of, a public official or any person in a position to influence a business outcome for MyMoolah.

## 5. Third-Party Due Diligence

### 5.1 Obligations

All third parties acting on behalf of or in association with MyMoolah — including agents, intermediaries, introducers, suppliers, and technology partners — must be subject to proportionate anti-bribery and corruption due diligence before engagement and on an ongoing basis.

### 5.2 Due Diligence Procedures

1. **Risk Assessment**: Each proposed third-party relationship must be assessed for bribery and corruption risk, considering the nature of services, geography, sector, involvement of public officials, and value of the engagement.
2. **Background Checks**: Appropriate background checks must be conducted, including adverse media screening, sanctions screening, and verification of beneficial ownership.
3. **Contractual Protections**: All third-party contracts must include anti-bribery and corruption clauses, rights of audit, and termination provisions for breach.
4. **Ongoing Monitoring**: Third-party relationships must be subject to periodic review and monitoring proportionate to the assessed risk level.

### 5.3 Red Flags

The following are indicators that warrant enhanced scrutiny:

- Requests for unusual payment arrangements (offshore accounts, payments to unrelated parties, cash).
- Refusal to agree to anti-bribery contractual provisions.
- The third party is a shell company or has opaque ownership structures.
- Connections to public officials or politically exposed persons.
- Requests for commissions or fees that are disproportionate to the services rendered.
- Operations in jurisdictions with high perceived corruption (per Transparency International Corruption Perceptions Index).
- Lack of a verifiable track record or legitimate business premises.
- Engagement was recommended by a public official or person in a position to influence a business outcome.

## 6. Reporting

### 6.1 Obligation to Report

All persons subject to this policy have an obligation to report any known or suspected instance of bribery or corruption immediately. Failure to report is itself a disciplinary offence.

### 6.2 Reporting Channels

| Channel | Contact |
|---------|---------|
| Direct line manager | In person or in writing |
| Chief Compliance Officer | compliance@mymoolah.africa |
| Board Chairperson | Via Company Secretary |
| Anonymous hotline | To be established; details published on intranet |
| External (if internal channels exhausted or inappropriate) | Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC), South African Police Service (SAPS), Public Protector |

Reports may be made anonymously. All reports will be treated with the strictest confidence, subject to legal and regulatory obligations.

### 6.3 Protection from Retaliation

MyMoolah prohibits retaliation against any person who, in good faith, reports a concern under this policy. Protections under the Protected Disclosures Act 26 of 2000 apply. Any person found to have retaliated against a reporter shall be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal.

## 7. Investigation Procedures

1. All reports will be acknowledged within five (5) business days and assigned to a designated investigator.
2. Investigations will be conducted impartially, thoroughly, and with due regard for confidentiality and the rights of all parties.
3. Where the report concerns a member of the Compliance Department or senior management, the investigation shall be escalated to the Board or an independent external investigator.
4. The outcome of the investigation shall be documented and reported to the Chief Compliance Officer and, where appropriate, to the Board.
5. Where an investigation reveals conduct that may constitute a criminal offence, the matter shall be reported to the South African Police Service and, where applicable, the Financial Intelligence Centre.

## 8. Disciplinary Action

Any person found to have engaged in bribery, corruption, or a breach of this policy shall be subject to disciplinary action, which may include:

- Formal written warning.
- Suspension pending investigation.
- Summary dismissal for gross misconduct.
- Recovery of any financial loss suffered by the Company.
- Referral to law enforcement authorities for criminal prosecution.
- Termination of contractual relationship (for third parties).

## 9. Training

All employees and directors must complete anti-bribery and corruption training within thirty (30) days of commencement of employment or appointment and annually thereafter. Training records shall be maintained by the Compliance Department. Third parties in higher-risk categories shall receive appropriate training or be required to demonstrate equivalent internal training programmes.

## 10. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Board of Directors** | Overall accountability for the anti-bribery and corruption framework. Approve this policy and ensure adequate resources for its implementation. |
| **Chief Executive Officer** | Ensure that the tone from the top reflects zero tolerance for bribery and corruption. Approve charitable donations and sponsorships. |
| **Chief Compliance Officer** | Develop, implement, and monitor this policy. Maintain registers. Oversee training. Receive and manage reports. Report to the Board. |
| **Line Managers** | Ensure awareness and compliance within their teams. Escalate concerns promptly. |
| **All Employees** | Comply with this policy. Report concerns. Complete training. Maintain records of gifts and hospitality. |
| **Third Parties** | Comply with anti-bribery contractual obligations. Cooperate with due diligence and audit requests. |

## 11. Monitoring and Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually, or sooner if required by changes in legislation, regulation, business operations, or following a significant incident. The Chief Compliance Officer shall report to the Board at least annually on the effectiveness of the anti-bribery and corruption framework, including:

- Number and nature of reports received.
- Outcomes of investigations.
- Training completion rates.
- Results of third-party due diligence reviews.
- Any changes to the risk environment.
