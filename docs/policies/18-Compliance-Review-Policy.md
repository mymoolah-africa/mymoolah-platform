## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework through which MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd ("MyMoolah") conducts independent reviews and audits of its compliance function, regulatory obligations, and internal controls. It ensures that the effectiveness of MyMoolah's compliance programme is objectively assessed, that deficiencies are identified and remediated in a timely manner, and that the Board of Directors receives reliable assurance on the state of compliance across all operations.

Independent compliance review is a cornerstone of sound governance in financial services. Section 42C of the Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001 (FICA) requires accountable institutions to establish and maintain a compliance function. King IV Principle 15 advocates a combined assurance model to provide the governing body with a comprehensive view of the organisation's risk and compliance posture. This policy operationalises these requirements within the context of MyMoolah's digital wallet and payment platform.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All compliance review and audit activities conducted by, or on behalf of, MyMoolah.
- All aspects of MyMoolah's compliance programme, including but not limited to AML/CFT controls, KYC/CDD procedures, transaction monitoring, sanctions screening, data protection, information security, and regulatory reporting.
- Internal compliance reviews conducted by MyMoolah's Compliance function.
- External compliance reviews conducted by independent third-party auditors or specialists.
- Regulatory examinations and inspections by the SARB, FSCA, FIC, or other competent authorities.
- All departments, business units, and personnel whose activities fall within the scope of regulatory compliance obligations.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Independent Compliance Review** | A systematic, objective assessment of the design and operating effectiveness of MyMoolah's compliance controls, conducted by persons independent of the function under review. |
| **Internal Review** | A compliance review conducted by MyMoolah's internal audit function or by compliance personnel independent of the area under review. |
| **External Review** | A compliance review conducted by an independent third-party firm with relevant qualifications and experience. |
| **Combined Assurance** | An integrated approach combining the assurance provided by management (first line), risk and compliance functions (second line), and internal/external audit (third line) to provide a comprehensive view of the organisation's risk posture. |
| **Finding** | A deficiency, weakness, or non-compliance identified during a review or audit. |
| **Remediation** | Corrective action taken to address a finding and prevent its recurrence. |
| **Quality Assurance** | Ongoing and periodic assessments of the compliance function's own effectiveness, processes, and outputs. |
| **CDD** | Customer Due Diligence. |
| **EDD** | Enhanced Due Diligence. |
| **STR** | Suspicious Transaction Report. |

## 4. Review Programme

### 4.1 Annual Review Plan

The Chief Compliance Officer, in consultation with the Chief Risk Officer and the Audit Committee, shall develop an annual compliance review plan. The plan shall:

- Be risk-based, prioritising areas of highest regulatory risk and operational significance.
- Cover all material compliance domains over a rolling three-year cycle at minimum.
- Include both internal and external review activities.
- Be approved by the Audit Committee before the commencement of each calendar year.
- Be flexible to accommodate ad-hoc reviews triggered by regulatory changes, incidents, or emerging risks.

### 4.2 Minimum Review Frequency

| Compliance Domain | Minimum Frequency | Review Type |
|---|---|---|
| AML/CFT programme effectiveness | Annual | Internal + External (alternating) |
| KYC/CDD quality and completeness | Semi-annual | Internal |
| Transaction monitoring calibration | Annual | External |
| Sanctions screening accuracy | Annual | Internal |
| Data protection (POPIA) compliance | Annual | Internal + External (alternating) |
| Information security controls | Annual | External (penetration testing + audit) |
| Regulatory reporting accuracy | Semi-annual | Internal |
| Compliance training effectiveness | Annual | Internal |
| Third-party/vendor compliance | Annual | Internal |
| Fraud prevention controls | Annual | Internal |

### 4.3 External Review Requirements

An independent external compliance review shall be conducted at least once every two years, covering the full scope of MyMoolah's compliance programme. The external review shall be mandatory annually for:

- AML/CFT programme effectiveness (in years where not covered by external review, a comprehensive internal review shall be conducted by personnel independent of the compliance function).
- Transaction monitoring system calibration and effectiveness.
- Information security controls (including penetration testing by a certified ethical hacking firm).

## 5. Reviewer Independence & Qualifications

### 5.1 Independence Requirements

- Internal reviewers shall not review processes or controls for which they have direct operational responsibility.
- Internal reviewers shall report their findings directly to the Audit Committee, with a copy to the Chief Compliance Officer.
- External reviewers shall have no commercial relationship with MyMoolah that could compromise their objectivity, beyond the review engagement itself.
- External reviewers shall confirm their independence in writing at the commencement of each engagement.

### 5.2 Qualification Standards

**Internal Reviewers:**
- Members of the internal audit function, or compliance personnel with relevant qualifications (CAMS, ICA, CIA, CISA, or equivalent).
- Minimum three years of experience in compliance, audit, or risk management within financial services.

**External Reviewers:**
- Registered audit firm, or specialist compliance/risk advisory firm with demonstrated financial services experience.
- Engagement lead shall hold relevant professional qualifications (CA(SA), CAMS, CIA, CISA, or equivalent).
- Demonstrated experience in reviewing digital financial services, payment platforms, or fintech compliance programmes.
- Familiarity with South African regulatory requirements (FICA, POPIA, FSCA, SARB).

## 6. Review Methodology

### 6.1 Standard Review Procedures

All compliance reviews shall follow a structured methodology incorporating the following elements:

**(a) Planning and Scoping**
- Definition of review objectives, scope, and criteria.
- Identification of key risks and control objectives within the scope.
- Selection of review sample and testing methodology.
- Timeline and resource allocation.

**(b) Document and Policy Review**
- Assessment of the currency, completeness, and adequacy of compliance policies, procedures, and guidelines.
- Evaluation of policy alignment with current regulatory requirements and industry standards.
- Review of policy approval, distribution, and acknowledgement records.

**(c) Sample Testing**
- Selection of a statistically significant or risk-based sample of transactions, customer files, reports, or other records.
- Testing of compliance controls against documented procedures and regulatory requirements.
- Minimum sample sizes:

| Population Size | Minimum Sample |
|---|---|
| < 100 | 25% or 20 items, whichever is greater |
| 100–500 | 50 items |
| 500–1,000 | 75 items |
| > 1,000 | 100 items or statistical sampling at 95% confidence level |

**(d) Interviews**
- Structured interviews with key personnel to assess awareness, understanding, and application of compliance requirements.
- Interview subjects shall include front-line staff, compliance personnel, management, and technology team members as relevant to the review scope.

**(e) Systems Testing**
- Technical assessment of automated compliance controls (transaction monitoring rules, sanctions screening algorithms, KYC verification workflows).
- Evaluation of system configuration, alert thresholds, and calibration effectiveness.
- Review of system audit trails, access controls, and data integrity mechanisms.
- Assessment of GCP infrastructure security controls (Cloud Run, Cloud SQL, Secret Manager configurations).

**(f) Walkthrough Testing**
- End-to-end walkthrough of key compliance processes (e.g., customer onboarding, STR filing, sanctions hit resolution) to verify adherence to documented procedures.

## 7. Findings Classification

### 7.1 Classification Framework

All findings identified during compliance reviews shall be classified according to the following severity framework:

| Classification | Definition | Remediation Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | A finding that represents an immediate risk of material regulatory breach, significant financial loss, or systemic control failure. The finding requires urgent Board notification and immediate remedial action. | 30 calendar days |
| **Major** | A finding that represents a significant control weakness or regulatory non-compliance that could, if left unaddressed, result in regulatory sanction, material financial loss, or reputational damage. | 60 calendar days |
| **Minor** | A finding that represents a control deficiency or deviation from best practice that does not pose an immediate material risk but should be addressed to strengthen the compliance framework. | 90 calendar days |
| **Observation** | A finding that represents an opportunity for improvement or enhancement to the compliance programme, but does not constitute a control weakness or non-compliance. | Next annual review cycle |

### 7.2 Escalation Protocol

- Critical findings shall be reported to the Board Chair and Audit Committee Chair within 24 hours of identification.
- Major findings shall be reported to the Chief Compliance Officer and Chief Risk Officer within 48 hours of identification.
- All findings shall be included in the formal review report to the Audit Committee.

---

## 8. Remediation Tracking

### 8.1 Remediation Process

- For each finding, a remediation action plan shall be developed by the responsible business owner, with input from the Compliance function.
- The action plan shall specify: the root cause of the finding, the proposed corrective action, the responsible individual, the target completion date, and the evidence required to demonstrate remediation.
- Remediation plans for Critical and Major findings shall be approved by the Chief Compliance Officer before implementation.

### 8.2 Tracking and Follow-Up

- The Compliance function shall maintain a centralised findings tracker, recording all findings, classifications, remediation plans, target dates, and completion status.
- Progress on remediation shall be reported to the Compliance Committee on a monthly basis.
- Outstanding findings past their remediation deadline shall be escalated to the Audit Committee with an explanation and revised timeline.

### 8.3 Verification

- Remediation of Critical and Major findings shall be independently verified by the Compliance function or internal audit before closure.
- Verification shall confirm that the corrective action has been implemented effectively and that the underlying control weakness has been addressed.

## 9. Reporting

### 9.1 Review Reports

Each compliance review shall result in a formal written report containing:

- Executive summary with overall compliance assessment.
- Scope, methodology, and limitations of the review.
- Detailed findings with classification, evidence, and root cause analysis.
- Remediation recommendations and proposed action plans.
- Management response to each finding.
- Overall assessment of the compliance programme's effectiveness.

### 9.2 Reporting Cadence

| Report | Recipient | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Individual review reports | Chief Compliance Officer, Chief Risk Officer, Audit Committee | Upon completion of each review |
| Findings tracker summary | Compliance Committee | Monthly |
| Compliance review programme status | Audit Committee | Quarterly |
| Annual compliance assurance report | Board of Directors | Annual |

### 9.3 Regulatory Reporting

- Review findings that indicate a material breach of regulatory requirements shall be reported to the relevant regulator (SARB, FSCA, FIC) in accordance with regulatory notification obligations.
- Review reports, findings, and remediation records shall be made available to regulators upon request.

## 10. Regulatory Examination Preparedness

### 10.1 Preparedness Programme

MyMoolah shall maintain a state of continuous readiness for regulatory examinations by:

- Maintaining organised and accessible compliance documentation, policies, procedures, and records.
- Conducting annual mock regulatory examinations (internal or facilitated by external advisors).
- Designating a regulatory examination coordinator responsible for liaison with examiners.
- Maintaining a regulatory examination response plan, including document production protocols, interview preparation, and communication procedures.

### 10.2 Post-Examination Actions

- All findings from regulatory examinations shall be entered into the findings tracker and subject to the same remediation, tracking, and reporting procedures as internal and external review findings.
- A post-examination debrief shall be conducted within 14 calendar days of the examination's conclusion.

---

## 11. Quality Assurance of the Compliance Function

### 11.1 Internal Quality Assurance

The Compliance function shall conduct ongoing quality assurance of its own processes, including:

- Monthly quality review of STR filings (sample of 10% or 5 reports, whichever is greater).
- Quarterly quality review of KYC/CDD files processed by the compliance team.
- Semi-annual review of transaction monitoring alert disposition decisions.
- Annual self-assessment of compliance programme maturity against the FICA requirements and FATF standards.

### 11.2 External Quality Assurance

An independent external assessment of the compliance function's effectiveness shall be conducted at least once every three years, in line with the Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA) Standards for quality assurance.

## 12. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Board of Directors** | Approve the Compliance Review Policy; receive annual compliance assurance report; ensure adequate resources for the review programme. |
| **Audit Committee** | Oversee the compliance review programme; approve the annual review plan; receive and review all review reports; monitor remediation progress; recommend external reviewer appointments. |
| **Chief Compliance Officer** | Develop and execute the annual compliance review plan; manage internal and external review engagements; maintain the findings tracker; report to the Audit Committee; conduct quality assurance of the compliance function. |
| **Chief Risk Officer** | Provide risk assessment input to review planning; ensure alignment between compliance reviews and the enterprise risk management framework; co-review Critical findings. |
| **Internal Audit** | Conduct independent compliance reviews as assigned; provide third-line assurance; verify remediation of Critical and Major findings. |
| **Business Unit Heads** | Cooperate with reviewers; develop and implement remediation action plans; ensure timely completion of corrective actions. |
| **External Reviewers** | Conduct independent reviews in accordance with the engagement terms, professional standards, and this policy. |

## 13. Monitoring & Review

- This policy shall be reviewed annually, or upon material changes to regulatory requirements, MyMoolah's compliance programme, or the organisation's governance structure.
- The Audit Committee shall evaluate the effectiveness of the compliance review programme annually and recommend improvements.
- Changes to the review methodology, scope, or frequency shall be approved by the Audit Committee.
