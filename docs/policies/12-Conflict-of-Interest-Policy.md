## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for the identification, disclosure, management, and resolution of conflicts of interest within MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd ("the Company"). As a provider of banking-grade financial services — including digital wallet, value-added services, PayShap, USDC cross-border payments, NFC, KYC, and reconciliation operations — the Company holds positions of trust with clients, regulators, banking partners, and Mojaloop network participants. Conflicts of interest, whether actual, potential, or perceived, threaten that trust and must be managed with the highest standards of governance and transparency.

This policy gives effect to the requirements of the Companies Act 71 of 2008, with particular reference to Section 75 (directors' personal financial interests), and aligns with the governance principles set out in King IV.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All directors, officers, and employees of MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd.
- All contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and interns.
- All persons serving on committees, advisory boards, or working groups established by the Company.
- All third-party agents and intermediaries acting on behalf of the Company, to the extent that a conflict may arise in relation to their engagement.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Conflict of Interest** | A situation in which a person's private interests — financial, personal, or otherwise — diverge from, or may reasonably be perceived to diverge from, the interests of the Company, such that the person's ability to act objectively and in the best interests of the Company is, or may be, compromised. |
| **Actual Conflict** | A conflict that presently exists between a person's private interests and the interests of the Company. |
| **Potential Conflict** | A situation that could develop into an actual conflict of interest if certain foreseeable circumstances materialise. |
| **Perceived Conflict** | A situation that a reasonable, informed third party could conclude presents a conflict of interest, regardless of whether an actual conflict exists. |
| **Personal Financial Interest** | As defined in Section 1 of the Companies Act 71 of 2008: a direct material interest of that person, of a financial, monetary, or economic nature, or to which a monetary value may be attributed. |
| **Related Party** | A person connected to a director, officer, or employee by family relationship (spouse, life partner, child, parent, sibling), business relationship, or common financial interest. |
| **Register** | The Company's Conflict of Interest Register, maintained by the Compliance Department, in which all disclosed conflicts are recorded. |

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Overarching Principle

All persons within scope must act in the best interests of the Company and must not allow any personal interest — whether financial, familial, social, or otherwise — to improperly influence, or appear to influence, their judgement, decisions, or actions in relation to Company business.

### 4.2 Types of Conflict

The following are common categories of conflict of interest that may arise within a fintech environment. This list is illustrative, not exhaustive:

#### 4.2.1 Personal Financial Interests

- Holding a financial interest (shares, debt instruments, options, cryptocurrency) in a competitor, client, supplier, or partner of the Company.
- Deriving a personal financial benefit from a transaction or relationship to which the Company is a party.
- Trading on material non-public information obtained through one's role at the Company.

#### 4.2.2 Outside Employment and Business Activities

- Holding a directorship, partnership, or employment position with another entity where the duties or interests of that position conflict or may conflict with the person's duties to the Company.
- Engaging in freelance, consulting, or business activities that compete with, or relate to, the Company's services or client base.

#### 4.2.3 Family and Personal Relationships

- A family member or close personal associate is employed by, or has a business relationship with, a competitor, client, supplier, or partner of the Company.
- A family member or close personal associate is a candidate for employment, promotion, or a contractual engagement with the Company.
- Supervising or making employment decisions regarding a family member or close personal associate.

#### 4.2.4 Board and Committee Memberships

- Serving on the board, advisory body, or committee of another entity whose interests may conflict with those of the Company.
- Participating in governance decisions at the Company where the person, or a related party, has a personal financial interest in the outcome.

#### 4.2.5 Investment Conflicts

- Investing in, or advising on investments in, entities that transact with the Company or operate in the same market.
- Participating in initial coin offerings (ICOs), token launches, or cryptocurrency investments that relate to the Company's USDC or digital asset operations.

#### 4.2.6 Gifts and Hospitality

- Receiving gifts, hospitality, or benefits from a person or entity with which the Company has, or may reasonably be expected to have, a business relationship, where the gift or benefit could reasonably be perceived as intended to influence the recipient's judgement. The detailed framework for gifts and hospitality is set out in the Company's Anti-Bribery & Corruption Policy (Policy 09).

## 5. Disclosure Requirements

### 5.1 Obligation to Disclose

All persons within scope have an affirmative obligation to disclose any actual, potential, or perceived conflict of interest as soon as the person becomes aware of it. When in doubt, disclose.

### 5.2 How to Disclose

Disclosures must be made in writing to the Chief Compliance Officer using the Company's Conflict of Interest Declaration Form. The disclosure must include:

1. A description of the conflict or potential conflict.
2. The parties involved.
3. The nature and extent of any financial or personal interest.
4. Any steps the person has already taken or proposes to take to manage the conflict.

### 5.3 Directors' Obligations under the Companies Act

Section 75 of the Companies Act 71 of 2008 imposes specific obligations on directors in relation to personal financial interests:

1. A director who has a personal financial interest in respect of a matter to be considered at a meeting of the Board must disclose that interest to the Board before the matter is considered.
2. The director must disclose the interest and its general nature before the matter is considered, or as soon as the director becomes aware of the interest.
3. The disclosure must be recorded in the minutes of the meeting.
4. The director must recuse himself or herself from the discussion and may not vote on the matter, unless the Board determines that the interest is not material or is common to all directors.
5. A failure to comply with Section 75 may result in personal liability and the transaction being voidable.

### 5.4 Annual Declaration

All directors, officers, and employees at management level and above are required to complete an annual Conflict of Interest Declaration, even if the declaration is a nil return. Annual declarations shall be submitted by 31 March each year, or within thirty (30) days of appointment, whichever is later.

## 6. Management of Disclosed Conflicts

### 6.1 Assessment

Upon receipt of a disclosure, the Chief Compliance Officer shall assess the nature, materiality, and risk of the conflict and determine the appropriate management action.

### 6.2 Management Actions

Depending on the assessment, one or more of the following actions may be taken:

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **No action required** | The conflict is immaterial or does not pose a risk to the Company's interests. The disclosure is recorded and monitored. |
| **Recusal** | The conflicted person is excluded from all discussions, decisions, and access to information relating to the matter. |
| **Restriction** | The conflicted person's involvement in the matter is restricted (e.g., they may participate in discussions but not vote or make decisions). |
| **Divestment** | The conflicted person is required to divest themselves of the interest that gives rise to the conflict. |
| **Reassignment** | The conflicted person's duties are reassigned to another person who does not have a conflict. |
| **Enhanced monitoring** | Additional oversight measures are implemented to ensure that the conflicted person's involvement does not compromise the Company's interests. |
| **Termination of relationship** | In severe cases, the engagement or employment may be terminated. |

### 6.3 Documentation

All disclosures, assessments, and management actions shall be recorded in the Conflict of Interest Register and, where applicable, in the minutes of Board or committee meetings.

## 7. Related-Party Transactions

All transactions between the Company and a related party (as defined in this policy and in the Companies Act) must be conducted on arm's length terms and be subject to appropriate approval processes. The following requirements apply:

1. All related-party transactions must be disclosed to the Chief Compliance Officer before the transaction is entered into.
2. Transactions above a materiality threshold determined by the Board must receive Board approval, with the conflicted director recused.
3. Related-party transactions must be documented, including the commercial rationale and the basis on which arm's length terms were determined.
4. The Audit Committee shall review related-party transactions on at least a quarterly basis.

## 8. Specific Provisions for Board Members

In addition to their obligations under Section 75 of the Companies Act, directors of MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd shall:

1. Maintain a standing register of their directorships, financial interests, and related-party relationships, to be updated within seven (7) days of any material change.
2. Declare any interest at the commencement of each Board meeting.
3. Recuse themselves from any matter in which they have a personal financial interest, unless the Board, having been informed of the interest, determines that the interest is not material.
4. Not use their position or information obtained as a director to gain an undue advantage for themselves or any related party.
5. Comply with King IV Principles 1–3 regarding ethical leadership, organisational ethics, and responsible corporate citizenship.

## 9. Consequences of Non-Compliance

### 9.1 Failure to Disclose

Any person who fails to disclose a conflict of interest, or who provides a materially incomplete or misleading disclosure, shall be subject to disciplinary action, which may include:

- Formal written warning.
- Suspension pending investigation.
- Summary dismissal for gross misconduct.
- Recovery of any gain or profit derived from the undisclosed conflict.
- Referral to regulatory or law enforcement authorities where applicable.

### 9.2 Directors

A director who fails to comply with Section 75 of the Companies Act may be held personally liable for any loss suffered by the Company. The transaction in respect of which the non-disclosure occurred may be voidable at the instance of the Company.

### 9.3 Third Parties

Third parties who fail to disclose conflicts or who engage in conduct that gives rise to an undisclosed conflict may have their contract or engagement terminated with immediate effect.

## 10. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Board of Directors** | Ensure an effective conflict of interest framework is in place. Comply with Section 75 of the Companies Act. Lead by example. Review the Conflict of Interest Register. |
| **Chief Executive Officer** | Ensure that conflicts are managed proactively throughout the organisation. Disclose personal conflicts. |
| **Chief Compliance Officer** | Administer this policy. Maintain the Conflict of Interest Register. Assess disclosures. Advise on management actions. Report to the Board. |
| **Audit Committee** | Provide independent oversight of related-party transactions and the conflict of interest framework. |
| **Line Managers** | Ensure awareness within their teams. Escalate disclosures. Monitor compliance. |
| **All Employees** | Disclose conflicts promptly. Complete annual declarations. Seek guidance when uncertain. Cooperate with assessments. |

## 11. Monitoring and Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually, or sooner if required by changes in legislation, regulation, or business operations. The Chief Compliance Officer shall report to the Board at least annually on:

- The number and nature of conflicts disclosed.
- Management actions taken.
- Annual declaration completion rates.
- Related-party transactions reviewed.
- Any instances of undisclosed conflicts and the consequences applied.
- Recommendations for improvement.
