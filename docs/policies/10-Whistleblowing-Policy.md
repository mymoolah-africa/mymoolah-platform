## 1. Purpose

MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd ("the Company") is committed to conducting its business with the highest standards of integrity, transparency, and ethical conduct. This policy establishes a safe, accessible, and effective mechanism through which any person may report, in good faith, any suspected wrongdoing, irregularity, or improper conduct within or related to the Company's operations — without fear of retaliation, victimisation, or any form of occupational detriment.

As a provider of banking-grade financial services — including digital wallets, value-added services, PayShap, USDC cross-border payments, and KYC processes — the Company recognises that early detection of misconduct is essential to safeguarding its clients, its regulatory standing, and the integrity of the financial system.

This policy gives effect to the Company's obligations under the Protected Disclosures Act 26 of 2000 and aligns with the governance principles set out in King IV.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All directors, officers, and employees of MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd, regardless of seniority, function, or location.
- All contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and interns.
- All third-party agents, intermediaries, and business partners.
- Any other person who becomes aware of suspected wrongdoing in connection with the Company's affairs.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Protected Disclosure** | A disclosure of information regarding any conduct of an employer, or an employee of that employer, made by any employee who has reason to believe that the information disclosed shows or tends to show one or more of the irregularities set out in the Protected Disclosures Act 26 of 2000. |
| **Whistleblower** | Any person who makes or intends to make a protected disclosure in accordance with this policy. |
| **Improper Conduct** | Any conduct which, if proved, would constitute a criminal offence, a failure to comply with a legal obligation, a miscarriage of justice, an endangerment of health or safety, an act of environmental damage, unfair discrimination, or any deliberate concealment of any of the foregoing. |
| **Occupational Detriment** | Any act or omission by an employer or another person that adversely affects the employment or working conditions of an employee, including but not limited to disciplinary action, dismissal, suspension, demotion, transfer, harassment, intimidation, or any other form of disadvantage. |
| **Good Faith** | A report made honestly, with a reasonable belief that the information disclosed is substantially true, and without malicious intent. |
| **Retaliation** | Any adverse action taken against a whistleblower as a consequence of, or in response to, a protected disclosure. |

## 4. What Should Be Reported

Persons subject to this policy are encouraged and expected to report any conduct they reasonably believe constitutes or may constitute:

1. A criminal offence (including fraud, theft, bribery, corruption, money laundering, or terrorist financing).
2. A failure to comply with any legal or regulatory obligation.
3. A miscarriage of justice.
4. A danger to the health or safety of any individual.
5. Damage to the environment.
6. Unfair discrimination as contemplated in the Promotion of Equality and Prevention of Unfair Discrimination Act 4 of 2000.
7. A breach of the Company's policies, procedures, or code of ethics.
8. Financial mismanagement, misreporting, or irregularity.
9. A failure to comply with anti-money laundering or counter-terrorist financing obligations.
10. An abuse of authority or position.
11. A deliberate concealment of any of the above.

This is not an exhaustive list. When in doubt, individuals are encouraged to report. The Company would rather investigate a report that proves unfounded than have improper conduct go unreported.

## 5. Reporting Channels

### 5.1 Internal Channels

| Channel | How to Report | When to Use |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Direct Line Manager** | In person, in writing, or by email | First point of contact for most concerns, unless the concern involves the manager. |
| **Chief Compliance Officer** | compliance@mymoolah.africa | For concerns involving regulatory breaches, financial crime, or where the line manager is involved or the reporter is uncomfortable reporting to them. |
| **Chief Executive Officer** | Via Company Secretary | For concerns involving senior management. |
| **Board Chairperson** | Via Company Secretary | For concerns involving the CEO, executive management, or matters of governance. |
| **Audit Committee** | Via Company Secretary | For concerns relating to financial reporting, audit, or accounting irregularities. |

### 5.2 Anonymous Reporting

The Company provides an anonymous reporting mechanism to enable reports where the whistleblower does not wish to identify themselves. Anonymous reports will be investigated with the same rigour as attributed reports, although anonymity may limit the Company's ability to investigate fully or to provide feedback.

The anonymous reporting facility shall be clearly communicated to all employees and shall be accessible via the Company's intranet and employee handbook.

### 5.3 External Channels

Where a whistleblower has exhausted internal channels, or where internal reporting is inappropriate, impractical, or has failed to result in appropriate action, the following external channels are available:

| Channel | Contact | Basis |
|---------|---------|-------|
| **Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC)** | www.fic.gov.za | Financial crime, money laundering, terrorist financing |
| **South African Police Service (SAPS)** | Nearest police station or 10111 | Criminal conduct |
| **Public Protector** | www.pprotect.org | Improper conduct by state organs or public officials |
| **Relevant regulatory authority** | As applicable (e.g., SARB, FSCA) | Regulatory non-compliance |

Disclosures to external bodies are protected under the Protected Disclosures Act 26 of 2000 where the conditions set out in that Act are satisfied.

## 6. Protection from Retaliation

### 6.1 Commitment

MyMoolah is unreservedly committed to protecting whistleblowers from any form of retaliation, victimisation, or occupational detriment. This commitment extends to all persons within the scope of this policy.

### 6.2 Legal Protections

The Protected Disclosures Act 26 of 2000 provides that no employee may be subjected to any occupational detriment on account of having made a protected disclosure. An employee who is subjected to occupational detriment in breach of the Act may refer the matter to the appropriate court or tribunal for relief, including reinstatement and compensation.

### 6.3 Company Protections

In addition to the statutory protections, the Company undertakes that:

- No person who makes a report in good faith under this policy shall suffer any form of retaliation, whether formal or informal, direct or indirect.
- Any person found to have retaliated against a whistleblower shall be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including summary dismissal.
- Where a whistleblower believes they are being subjected to retaliation, they may report this to the Chief Compliance Officer or the Board Chairperson, and the matter will be treated as a priority.
- Interim protective measures (such as temporary reassignment or suspension of the alleged retaliator) may be implemented during an investigation.

### 6.4 Limitation

Protection under this policy does not extend to disclosures made in bad faith, with malicious intent, or with the knowledge that the information disclosed is false. Persons who make deliberately false or malicious reports may be subject to disciplinary action.

## 7. Investigation Process

### 7.1 Receipt and Acknowledgement

All reports received through any channel shall be acknowledged within five (5) business days of receipt. Where the report is made anonymously and no return contact is provided, the Company shall document the acknowledgement in its records.

### 7.2 Preliminary Assessment

The Chief Compliance Officer, or a delegated senior officer, shall conduct a preliminary assessment to determine whether the report falls within the scope of this policy and whether it warrants formal investigation. This assessment shall be completed within ten (10) business days.

### 7.3 Formal Investigation

Where a formal investigation is warranted:

1. An impartial investigator shall be appointed. Where the allegation involves the Compliance Department or senior management, an independent external investigator shall be engaged.
2. The investigation shall be conducted with due regard for fairness, confidentiality, and the rights of all parties, including the person against whom the allegation is made.
3. The investigator shall have the authority to access relevant documents, records, systems, and personnel, subject to applicable legal constraints.
4. The investigation shall be completed within a reasonable timeframe, typically within thirty (30) business days, unless complexity requires an extension.

### 7.4 Outcome and Action

Upon completion of the investigation:

1. The findings shall be documented in a written report.
2. The Chief Compliance Officer shall determine the appropriate course of action, which may include disciplinary proceedings, referral to law enforcement, process improvements, or no further action.
3. Where the findings reveal a systemic issue, the Compliance Department shall recommend remedial measures to prevent recurrence.
4. Where findings indicate a potential criminal offence, the matter shall be referred to the South African Police Service and, where applicable, the Financial Intelligence Centre.

## 8. Confidentiality

### 8.1 Obligations

The identity of the whistleblower shall be kept confidential to the greatest extent possible, consistent with the need to conduct a thorough investigation and comply with legal obligations. Information relating to a report shall be shared only with those persons who have a legitimate need to know in order to investigate or act upon the report.

### 8.2 Data Protection

All records relating to whistleblowing reports shall be processed and stored in compliance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 (POPIA).

## 9. Feedback to the Whistleblower

Where the whistleblower is identifiable and contactable, the Company shall provide:

- Acknowledgement of receipt within five (5) business days.
- An update on the progress of the investigation at reasonable intervals, unless doing so would compromise the investigation.
- Notification of the outcome of the investigation, to the extent permitted by law and the requirements of fairness to other parties.

## 10. Record Keeping

The Compliance Department shall maintain a confidential register of all whistleblowing reports received, including:

- Date of report and channel used.
- Nature of the allegation.
- Steps taken in the investigation.
- Outcome and actions taken.
- Any follow-up measures implemented.

Records shall be retained for a minimum of five (5) years from the date of closure of the matter, or longer where required by law or regulation.

## 11. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Board of Directors** | Ensure an effective whistleblowing framework is in place. Receive reports on whistleblowing activity. Ensure the Company culture supports safe reporting. |
| **Chief Executive Officer** | Set the tone from the top. Ensure all employees are aware of this policy and their right to report without fear. |
| **Chief Compliance Officer** | Administer this policy. Receive and manage reports. Oversee investigations. Maintain the whistleblowing register. Report to the Board. |
| **Audit Committee** | Provide independent oversight of the whistleblowing process. Review investigation outcomes and systemic issues. |
| **Line Managers** | Create an environment where concerns can be raised openly. Escalate reports received promptly and appropriately. Never discourage reporting. |
| **All Employees** | Report suspected wrongdoing. Cooperate with investigations. Maintain confidentiality. Refrain from retaliation. |

## 12. Policy Awareness and Training

All employees shall receive training on this policy at induction and annually thereafter. Training shall cover:

- The types of conduct that should be reported.
- The available reporting channels, including anonymous reporting.
- The protections available to whistleblowers.
- The consequences of retaliation.
- The investigation process and what to expect.

Training completion shall be recorded and monitored by the Compliance Department.

## 13. Monitoring and Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually, or sooner if required by changes in legislation, regulation, or following a significant incident. The Chief Compliance Officer shall report to the Board at least annually on:

- The number and nature of reports received.
- The outcomes of investigations.
- Any instances of retaliation and actions taken.
- Training completion rates.
- The effectiveness of reporting channels.
- Recommendations for improvement.
