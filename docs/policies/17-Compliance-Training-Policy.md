## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework through which MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd ("MyMoolah") ensures that all employees, contractors, directors, and officers possess the knowledge, skills, and awareness necessary to fulfil their compliance obligations. It governs the design, delivery, assessment, and monitoring of mandatory compliance training programmes across all business functions.

A well-informed workforce is the first line of defence against money laundering, terrorism financing, fraud, data breaches, and regulatory non-compliance. Section 42 of the Financial Intelligence Centre Act 38 of 2001 (FICA) imposes an explicit obligation on accountable institutions to train employees in the identification and reporting of suspicious and unusual transactions. This policy operationalises that obligation within the context of MyMoolah's digital wallet and payment platform operations.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All permanent employees of MyMoolah (Pty) Ltd, regardless of seniority or function.
- All temporary staff, contractors, and consultants who have access to MyMoolah systems, customer data, or financial processes.
- All directors and members of the Board of Directors.
- All personnel of third-party service providers who perform regulated functions on behalf of MyMoolah, to the extent contractually required.
- All training activities related to regulatory compliance, risk management, information security, data protection, anti-money laundering, counter-terrorism financing, fraud prevention, and ethical conduct.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Compliance Training** | Structured learning activities designed to ensure that personnel understand and can apply the laws, regulations, policies, and procedures relevant to their roles. |
| **Onboarding Training** | Mandatory compliance training delivered to all new employees within their first 30 calendar days of employment. |
| **Annual Refresher** | Mandatory training conducted once per calendar year to reinforce and update compliance knowledge. |
| **Role-Based Training** | Specialised training tailored to the specific compliance responsibilities of particular roles or functions. |
| **Ad-Hoc Training** | Training delivered outside the regular schedule in response to regulatory changes, new product launches, emerging risks, or compliance incidents. |
| **Assessment** | A formal evaluation (written test, scenario-based exercise, or practical demonstration) to measure comprehension and competence. |
| **STR** | Suspicious Transaction Report — a report filed with the Financial Intelligence Centre. |
| **goAML** | The FIC's online reporting platform for suspicious transaction and cash threshold reports. |
| **MLRO** | Money Laundering Reporting Officer. |
| **POPIA** | Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013. |

## 4. Training Programme Structure

### 4.1 Onboarding Training (All New Personnel)

All new employees, contractors, and directors shall complete the following onboarding compliance training within 30 calendar days of commencement:

| Module | Duration | Content Summary |
|---|---|---|
| MyMoolah Compliance Overview | 1 hour | Regulatory landscape; MyMoolah's compliance framework; key policies and procedures; reporting obligations. |
| AML/CFT Fundamentals | 2 hours | Money laundering stages; terrorism financing indicators; FICA obligations; customer due diligence principles; suspicious transaction identification; STR filing process. |
| Data Protection & POPIA | 1.5 hours | Personal information definition; lawful processing conditions; data subject rights; data breach response; POPIA obligations specific to digital financial services. |
| Fraud Awareness | 1 hour | Common fraud typologies (identity fraud, account takeover, social engineering, payment fraud); internal fraud indicators; reporting procedures. |
| Code of Ethics & Conduct | 1 hour | MyMoolah's ethical standards; conflicts of interest; gifts and hospitality; whistleblowing procedures; consequences of non-compliance. |
| Sanctions Compliance | 1 hour | South African, OFAC, EU, and UN sanctions regimes; screening obligations; prohibited dealings; escalation procedures. |
| Information Security Essentials | 1 hour | Password management; phishing awareness; device security; data classification; acceptable use of systems. |

### 4.2 Annual Refresher Training (All Personnel)

All personnel shall complete annual refresher training, to be delivered within the first quarter of each calendar year. The refresher programme shall cover:

- Updates to AML/CFT regulations, guidance, and typologies.
- Changes to MyMoolah's compliance policies and procedures.
- Lessons learned from compliance incidents, audit findings, and regulatory developments.
- Emerging risks relevant to MyMoolah's operations (crypto-asset regulation, AI in financial services, new fraud patterns).
- Reinforcement of suspicious transaction identification and reporting obligations.
- POPIA compliance updates and data protection best practices.

### 4.3 Role-Based Training

In addition to the general training programme, the following role-based training shall be provided:

**(a) Compliance Team**

| Module | Frequency | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Advanced AML/CFT | Semi-annual | Complex ML/TF typologies; trade-based laundering; crypto-asset laundering; cross-border risk; enhanced due diligence techniques. |
| STR Filing & goAML | Annual + ad-hoc | STR quality standards; goAML platform operation; FIC feedback analysis; SAR narrative writing; regulatory liaison. |
| Regulatory Change Management | Quarterly | Tracking and implementing regulatory amendments; impact assessments; policy update procedures. |
| Sanctions Deep Dive | Annual | Advanced sanctions screening; PEP identification; list management; circumvention techniques; case studies. |

**(b) Technology & Engineering Team**

| Module | Frequency | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Secure Coding Practices | Semi-annual | OWASP Top 10; input validation; parameterised queries; API security; cryptographic standards (AES-256-GCM, TLS 1.3). |
| Security Awareness — Technical | Annual | Penetration testing awareness; vulnerability management; secure deployment (GCP Cloud Run); secrets management (GCP Secret Manager). |
| Incident Response — Technical | Annual | Security incident identification; forensic evidence preservation; containment procedures; communication protocols. |
| Data Protection by Design | Annual | POPIA technical requirements; data minimisation; pseudonymisation; encryption at rest and in transit; data retention automation. |

**(c) Customer-Facing Personnel**

| Module | Frequency | Content |
|---|---|---|
| KYC/CDD Procedures | Semi-annual | Identity verification processes; document authentication; EDD triggers; ongoing monitoring obligations; PEP screening. |
| Suspicious Activity Recognition | Quarterly | Red flag indicators for digital wallet transactions; unusual PayShap patterns; USDC conversion anomalies; structuring behaviours. |
| Customer Communication | Annual | Appropriate disclosure limitations; tipping-off prohibition under FICA S29; complaint handling; escalation procedures. |

**(d) Management & Board**

| Module | Frequency | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Governance & Oversight | Annual | King IV governance principles; director duties under the Companies Act; fiduciary obligations; liability for compliance failures. |
| Risk Appetite & Tolerance | Annual | Understanding risk appetite frameworks; interpreting key risk indicators; decision-making within risk boundaries. |
| Regulatory Obligations | Semi-annual | Regulatory landscape for fintech/digital banking in South Africa; SARB expectations; FSCA conduct requirements; FICA S42C compliance officer function. |
| Emerging Risks | Annual | Crypto-asset regulation (FSCA declaration); AI governance; open banking; competition law; cross-border regulatory developments. |

## 5. Training Delivery Methods

### 5.1 Delivery Channels

Training shall be delivered through a combination of the following methods to maximise engagement, accessibility, and retention:

| Method | Use Case | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **E-Learning Platform** | Core compliance modules; onboarding; annual refresher | Self-paced online courses with embedded assessments |
| **Live Workshops** | Role-based training; complex topics; case study analysis | Instructor-led sessions (in-person or virtual) |
| **Scenario-Based Exercises** | AML/CFT training; fraud awareness; incident response | Interactive case studies with decision-point analysis |
| **Micro-Learning** | Regulatory updates; ad-hoc awareness campaigns | Short-form content (5–10 minutes); email bulletins; internal notifications |
| **Practical Demonstrations** | goAML filing; system-specific procedures; security tools | Hands-on guided exercises in sandbox environments |

### 5.2 Accessibility

All training materials shall be accessible to personnel with disabilities, in compliance with applicable accessibility standards. Training shall be available in English, with additional language support where required to ensure comprehension.

## 6. Assessment & Pass Requirements

### 6.1 Assessment Standards

- All compliance training modules shall include a formal assessment to evaluate comprehension and competence.
- Assessments may take the form of written tests, scenario-based evaluations, practical demonstrations, or oral examinations.
- A minimum pass mark of **80%** is required for all compliance training assessments.

### 6.2 Remediation

- Personnel who fail to achieve the minimum pass mark shall be required to retake the training module and assessment within 14 calendar days.
- A maximum of two re-assessment attempts shall be permitted per module per training cycle.
- Persistent failure to achieve the pass mark shall be escalated to the employee's line manager and the Chief Compliance Officer for remediation action, which may include supervised mentoring, additional training, or disciplinary procedures.

### 6.3 Consequences of Non-Completion

- Failure to complete mandatory training within the stipulated timeframe shall be recorded as a compliance breach.
- Repeated non-completion may result in disciplinary action, restriction of system access, or termination of employment or contract.
- Non-completion records shall be reported to the Compliance Committee on a monthly basis.

## 7. Training Records & Retention

### 7.1 Record-Keeping

The Compliance function shall maintain comprehensive training records, including:

- Name, employee ID, and role of each participant.
- Training module title, version, and delivery date.
- Assessment results, including pass/fail status and scores.
- Remediation actions taken for failed assessments.
- Trainer/facilitator details for instructor-led sessions.
- Training completion certificates.

### 7.2 Retention Period

- All training records shall be retained for a minimum of **five (5) years** from the date of training, in accordance with FICA record-keeping requirements.
- Records relating to personnel involved in suspicious transaction reporting shall be retained for the duration of the reporting obligation plus five years.

### 7.3 Regulatory Access

Training records shall be made available to the Financial Intelligence Centre, SARB, FSCA, or other regulatory authorities upon request.

## 8. Effectiveness Measurement

### 8.1 Key Performance Indicators

The effectiveness of the training programme shall be measured through the following indicators:

| Indicator | Target | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Training completion rate (onboarding) | 100% within 30 days | Monthly |
| Training completion rate (annual refresher) | 100% by end of Q1 | Quarterly |
| Assessment pass rate (first attempt) | ≥ 90% | Per training cycle |
| Average assessment score | ≥ 85% | Per training cycle |
| STR quality score (post-training) | ≥ 90% acceptance rate by FIC | Quarterly |
| Compliance incident rate (post-training) | Year-on-year reduction | Annual |
| Employee training satisfaction score | ≥ 4.0 / 5.0 | Per training cycle |

### 8.2 Continuous Improvement

- Training content and delivery methods shall be reviewed annually based on effectiveness metrics, participant feedback, audit findings, and regulatory developments.
- Lessons learned from compliance incidents, regulatory examinations, and industry developments shall be incorporated into training materials within 60 days of identification.

## 9. Ad-Hoc Training

### 9.1 Triggers for Ad-Hoc Training

Ad-hoc training shall be conducted in response to:

- Material changes to legislation or regulation (e.g., amendments to FICA, new FSCA directives, crypto-asset regulation updates).
- Launch of new products or services (e.g., new VAS offerings, new payment corridors, new vendor integrations).
- Identification of emerging risks or new fraud/ML/TF typologies.
- Significant compliance incidents or near-misses.
- Findings from internal or external audits requiring awareness or behavioural change.
- Regulatory guidance or circulars requiring operational response.

### 9.2 Delivery Timeframe

Ad-hoc training shall be designed and delivered within 30 calendar days of the trigger event, or within such shorter timeframe as the nature of the risk demands.

## 10. Board Training

### 10.1 Board Governance Training

Members of the Board of Directors shall receive dedicated training on:

- Their fiduciary and statutory obligations under the Companies Act 71 of 2008.
- King IV governance principles, particularly Principle 2 (governance of ethics) and Principle 11 (risk governance).
- AML/CFT regulatory obligations and the Board's oversight responsibilities under FICA.
- Data protection obligations under POPIA.
- Emerging regulatory risks relevant to fintech and digital financial services.
- Liability for compliance failures and regulatory sanctions.

### 10.2 Frequency

Board compliance training shall be conducted at least annually, with supplementary briefings provided as required in response to material regulatory developments.
